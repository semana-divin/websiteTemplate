As you can see, we're still a young generation with more goals to achieve. Once upon a time in high school, three students named Eloge, Divin, and Kelly crossed paths. We were all pursuing degrees in Software Development and happened to be classmates in several courses. Initially, we were just acquaintances, but as we collaborated on various projects and studied together, we grew closer. Over time, we found ourselves enjoying each other's company outside of the classroom as well. We discovered shared hobbies and interests, and our friendship blossomed into a great bond. Our bond deepened as we supported each other through the challenges of high school life and celebrated each other's success. As the school days passed, we met two other girls named Afrika Brunette and Gashugi M. Liliose, along with one boy named Bagabo P. Blessing. They matched our bond and vibes so well that we decided they were going to be part of our group. During the holidays, we stayed in touch with each other, and we created a group on Instagram to share different ideas. That's how we started building an unbreakable bond among the six of us. After the holidays, we had to complete our internship at IDA Technology to gain more experience and knowledge about software development. One day after our internship, we went to a restaurant, had lunch together, and took some pictures to capture the memories. Despite the distance, we were determined to stay connected. Over the next month back at school after our internship, our friendship flourished. We became inseparable, studying for exams side by side and supporting each other's extracurricular activities. Whenever one faced a challenge, the others were there, offering a listening ear. During lunch hour, we started discussing our dreams and the kinds of projects we could create as software developers. Our conversations expanded beyond school, and we became open to each other, sharing personal struggles and fears. Afrika Brunette brought up the idea of making our own website as a starting point in software development, and we all agreed. We started building it and shared different tasks to speed up the process. There were occasional disagreements and misunderstandings, but our bond was strong enough to weather these storms. We learned to communicate openly, apologize when needed, and grow together through life's challenges. In our hearts, we knew that the seeds of our friendship sown during our high school days had blossomed into an unbreakable bond that would last a lifetime. We were more than friends; we were soulmates who had found each other amidst the canvas of life.
